Popular Destinations

Nayon Pilipino Clark

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

A cultural destination showcasing Filipino heritage, traditions, architecture, and local arts through exhibits and themed attractions.

Mount Arayat

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

An iconic dormant volcano famous for hiking trails, forests, wildlife, and strong cultural significance in Pampanga folklore.

Palakol River

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

A peaceful river destination known for its natural scenery, fishing activities, and importance to nearby farming and local communities.

Aqua Planet

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

A popular water park offering thrilling slides, wave pools, and fun attractions for families and tourists.

Must-Try Food Places

Aling Lucing's Sisig

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

Known as the "Sisig Capital," this is where the original sisig became popular.

Apag Marangle

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

Apag Marangle is one of Pampanga’s most beloved Kapampangan restaurants, and yes — they have a branch in Angeles City aside from their original site in Guagua.

Fabrika Dining Villas

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

Fabrika Dining Villas is a charming restaurant offering a delightful mix of local and international cuisine, perfect for a romantic dinner or a special occasion.

Bale Capampangan

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

Bale Capampangan is a rustic-themed restaurant showcasing authentic Kapampangan cuisine, served in a homey setting that highlights Pampanga’s culinary heritage.

Upcoming Events and Festivals

La Naval Fiesta

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

A Catholic religious celebration honoring Our Lady of La Naval through solemn masses and processions, reflecting deep devotion among residents.

Masantol Seafood Festival

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

A celebration of the town’s fishing industry featuring seafood dishes and culinary showcases.

Lubao International Balloon & Music Festival

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

A large event combining hot air balloon displays, concerts, and tourism activities.

Giant Lantern Festival

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

Pampanga’s most famous festival featuring giant lanterns with synchronized lights and intricate designs competing every Christmas season.

Local Products and Delicacies

Angeles Bringhe

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

A coconut milk-based version of paella.

Bacolor Turrones de Casoy

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

A popular Kapampangan sweet made with cashew nuts, popular for pasalubong.

Guagua Chicharon

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

Crispy, freshly fried pork cracklings sold in local markets and roadside stalls.

Mexico Suman sa Lihia

☆☆☆☆☆ 0.0

Sticky rice soaked in lye water, wrapped in banana leaves, and steamed; popular in Brgy. San Juan and Brgy. San Isidro.
